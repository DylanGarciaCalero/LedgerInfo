Budgeting your money

Budgeting is essential for making the most of your money. It is a forecasted sum to be spent within a specified category and a plan for every dollar you have and spend. Budgeting is a system that involves putting away some money on a particular day, week, or month, and sticking to it to accumulate extra money in savings.

There are many different styles of budgeting. Some involve putting away money once a month, and others require you to put away a small amount of money every day. Refer to Table 3: Budgeting Types for suggestions on budget styles. 

 

Budget Type

Details

Suggested Savings Level

The 50/30/20 Budget

This budget divides spending into three allotments:

  • 50% to needs
  • 30% to wants
  • 20% to savings

Higher savings level. If less than 50% of your monthly income goes towards rent or mortgage, use this method.

The 70/20/10 Budget

This budget divides spending into three allotments:

  • 70% to needs
  • 20% to wants
  • 10% to savings

Middle savings level. If around 70% of your monthly income goes towards rent, mortgage, or transportation, use this method.

The 85/10/5 Budget

This budget divides spending into three allotments:

  • 85% to needs
  • 10% to wants
  • 5% to savings

Lower savings level. If a large majority of your monthly income goes towards rent, mortgage, or transportation, use this method.

Table 3: Budgeting Types.
